About Me

My name is Johan Philip and I am a second year Computer Science student at TMU.

My university journey began as a Bachelor of Health Sciences student at Ontario Tech University in Oshawa with the hopes of becoming a doctor or a physical therapist. However during my winter semester of 1st year Biology, we did a lab in which bioinformatics was the topic of discussion. Upon researching and learning more about the field, I discovered how interconnected Computer Science and healthcare was and how Computer Scientists could do far more than being Software Developers or Cybersecurity Analysts.

So I made the decision to transfer and begin doing Computer Science at TMU. While doing my first year courses, I began to find Software Development and Object-Oriented Programming very fascinating and began teaching myself Web Development and building projects. My decision to take CPS530 stemmed from a desire to ensure that the knowledge I have is correct and to fill in gaps in my knowledge.

When speaking more about my personal life, I was born in India. I lived in Kuwait till I was 7 when my family and I moved to Canada. For the past 14 years, I have called the great town of Ajax my home.

Coding & I

Having taken a programming class in grade 10 that taught me Turing, I found programming to be extremely boring and tedious and not at all something I wanted to do for a career. Being an active person who enjoyed working with my hands and being on the move, as well as someone who loved Biology and Science, I wanted to be become a doctor or a physiotherapist.

However, as fate would have it, I ended up finding my way back to programming as I mentioned in the previous section. As such, my late start to programming has meant that I have felt like I am quite behind in my knowledge, abilities and career preparedness (projects, etc) in comparison to my colleagues.

However, my new found passion for Software Development has spurred me to begin building an Inventory Management system for my current part-time workplace, a walk-in clinic.

The Inventory System will help ease the burden on my manager and provide a better alternative to the current inventory practice, which is just writting things down on paper. As this is my first major project, I have ran in several bugs and issues that have taught me much more about software and web development.

Upon completion of that project and ensuring that it works as intended, I hope to create an appointment scheduling system that will help move the clinic away from using pen and paper for appointment registration and truly digitize our patient intake process. I am hoping that through this process I will learn further skills about software developemnt whilst also creating technology that solves a real world problem.
